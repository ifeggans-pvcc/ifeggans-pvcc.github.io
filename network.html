Chapter 4 Terms
Term Definition
Network Classifications:
  • LAN:
  • MAN:
  • WAN:
  • Also known as a local area network, a LAN normally consists of a collection of computers in a single building or building complex.
  • Also known as metropolitan area network, MAN is a network of intermediate size.
  • Also known as a wide are network, WAN links machines over a greater distance.
Network Model:
  • Bus Network:
  • Star Network:
  • When the machines are all connected to a common communication line called a bus.
  • When one machine serve as a central focal point to all others that are connected.
Protocols:
  • CSMA/CD Ethernet:
  • CSMA/CA Wifi:
  • The right to transmit messages is controlled by the protocol, in a bus network based on the Ethernet standards.
  • When wireless networks adopt the policy of trying to avoid collisions rather than trying to detect them.
Combining Networks:
  • Repeater:
  • Bridges:
  • Switch:
  • Router:
  • Gateway:
  • A device that passes signals back and forth between the two original buses, without considering the meaning of the signals.
  • A birdge connects two buses, but it does not pass all messages across the connection.
  • A bridge with multiple connections, allowing it to connect several buses rather than two.
  • The device that handles the connection between networks to form an internet.
Client/Server Model
  • P2P(peer-to-peer):
  • Distributed System:
  • Cloud Computing:
  • Where the client/server model involves one process providing a service to numerous others.
  • Software units that execute as processes on different computers.
  • Where huge pools of shared computers on the network can be allocated for use of clients.
Internet Addressing:
  • Tier 1:
  • Tier 2:
  • Tier 3:
  • ISP's that consist of very high-speed, high-capacity, international WANs.(Back bone of the internet)
  • ISP's that tend to be more regional in scope and less potent in their capabilities.
  • An access ISP is, an independent Internet.
IP Address:
  • ICANN:
  • DNS:
  • TLD:
  • Country-Code TLDs(link):
  • A nonprofit corporation established to coordinate the Internet's operation.
  • An Internet-wide directory system, that the name of servers are used in.
  • Top-level domains, are the suffixes used to reflect their domains classification.
  • Two letter TLD's for each specific country
Internet Protocols:
  • FTP:
  • HTTP:
  • SMTP:
  • An application for listing and copying files across the network.(File transfer protocol)
  • Through the hyper text transfer protocol, more traditional network applications are handled.
  • Defines a way that 2 computers on the network interact, when transmitting an email from one host to another.
World Wide Web:
  • URL:
  • Client-side activities:
  • Server-side activities:
  • A unique address(Uniform Resource Locator).
  • If they are performed by a client.
  • If they are performed by a server.
Internet Four-layer Software:
  • Application Layer:
  • Transport Layer:
  • Network Layer:
  • Link Layer:
  • Where the message originates.
  • Collects packets and reassembles messages.
  • Detects that packet has reached its final destination.
  • Recieves packet.
Internet Is:
  • Packet-switching Network:
  • Fault Tolerant:
  • Redundant:
  • Digitial data is sent by breaking the data into blcoks of bits called packets, which contain both the data being transmitted and control information for routing the data.
  • The cumulative result of features for transporting, routing, and forwarding packet.
  • When congestion or failure occur.
CyberSecurity:
  • Malware:
  • Virus:
  • Worm:
  • Trojan Horse:
  • Spyware:
  • Phishing:
  • DDos Attack:
  • Malicious software.
  • Software that infects a computer by inserting itself into programs that already reside in the machine.
  • An Autonomous program that transfers itself through a network, taking up residence in computers and forwarding copies of itself to other computers.
  • A program that inserts a computer system disguised as a desirable program.
  • Software that collects information about activities on the computer an which it resides and reports that information back to the instigator of the attack.
  • A technique of obtaining information explicity by simply asking for it.
  • A distributed denial of service is when the work of generating the attacks is distributed over many hosts.
Protection:
  • Firewall:
  • Spam Filter:
  • Proxy Server:
  • Anti-virus Software:
  • Cryptography:
  • Public Key Encryption:
  • A primary prevention technique is to filter traffic passing through a point in the network.
  • Firewalls designed to block unwated email.
  • A software unit that acts as an intermediary between a client and a server, with the goal of shielding the client from attacks.
  • Another software used for defense against invasions via network connections.
  • The study of sending and recieving messages securely in the presence of adversaries.
  • Involves techniques where the encryption and decryption algorithms are known, and one of the keys is known, but the encoded message remains secure.
